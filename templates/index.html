Your penchant for travel is partly to blame for putting the environment in peril. Commercial flying accounts for about 3% of global carbon emissions, and that percentage is likely to increase as more people turn to the skies for travel.

But while passenger numbers are growing by 5% each year, airlines have succeeded in keeping their emission percentages to approximately half of that.

Most Sustainable Airlines for Flying Green:

  • EasyJet: British low-cost carrier, aiming to be the world's most carbon-efficient airline.
  • KLM: Dutch airline investing in sustainable aviation fuel and setting sustainability goals.
  • Lufthansa Group: Investing in fuel-efficient planes and researching sustainable aviation fuels.
  • United Airlines: American carrier committed to reducing emissions by 50% by 2050.
  • Air France: Working to reduce carbon footprint and investing in fuel-efficient aircraft.
  • Alaska Airlines: Recognized for sustainability initiatives, improving fuel efficiency, and reducing waste.
  • Etihad Airways: UAE-based airline investing in sustainability initiatives and more fuel-efficient aircraft.
  • Qantas: Australian airline committed to net zero emissions by 2050 and investing in sustainable aviation fuel.
  • Finnair: Finnish carrier committed to reducing emissions and improving sustainability.
  • Southwest Airlines: Recognized for conservation efforts, recycling cabin waste, and operating fuel-efficient aircraft.
